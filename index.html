A searchable index · built on Foster's Alumni Oxonienses

Members of the University of Oxford, 1500–1714

A finding aid for the prosopographical study of Oxford's colleges: reconstruct the cohort of any college or hall across a chosen span of years — every member admitted, ranked by the fullness of what Joseph Foster recorded of them — or search the whole University for an individual by name. The text is the double-rekeyed edition of Foster at British History Online.

Any result set can be downloaded as CSV for analysis, and every search is written into the page address, so a query — a college cohort, a family, a name — can be cited or shared as a stable link. Most members entered by matriculation; fellows, Students, demies, scholars, choristers and the like are dated by that admission and labelled.

Members of the University of Oxford, 1500–1714. A search interface to Joseph Foster, Alumni Oxonienses: The Members of the University of Oxford, 1500–1714 (Oxford, 1891–92). Reproduces the double-rekeyed text of British History Online (Institute of Historical Research), used by permission, including Foster’s Addenda and Corrigenda. Interface and dataset prepared by Jacob Donald Chatterjee, New College, Oxford. Before the matriculation register begins in 1565, members appear by their college admissions (fellow, demy, scholar, chorister, Student and the like); thereafter year is of matriculation, or for members admitted by other routes (fellow, Student, demy, scholar, chorister, servitor, and the like) of that admission, shown as “adm.” with the route named. Ordering is by length of Foster's entry, a proxy for the amount recorded, not for historical importance. Name search also reaches members with no recorded admission year (degree-only men, incorporations from Cambridge, and cross-references), listed separately.
